Common Threads in Your Module 8 Work

What's working

  • In your various assignments for this module, you hold GLAAD's business framing and Chapter 6's social justice framing in genuine tension, rather than picking one side
  • Each piece self-critiques its own case study rather than treating representation as an unambiguous win

Where to push further

  • Sharpen the core thesis early, so representation and business analysis feel unified rather than sequential
  • Trade general industry claims for hard numbers — sales figures, cost data, SRI scores specific to the studio in question
  • Name the creators/cast behind each project when relevant to the representation argument (e.g., Heartstopper's AroAce creator)

The Throughline

Whether it's Nick Nelson's coming-out arc, Elphaba's green skin, or the streaming economics behind Heated Rivalry, this module's real lesson is the same one Laverne Cox's story makes literal: representation is never just a headcount.

It's a set of choices about whose story gets the runtime, whose identity gets to be complicated, and whose visibility survives when the political weather shifts.
