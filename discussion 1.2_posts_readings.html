Symbolic Annihilation

"Through absence, condemnation, and trivialization, the media reflect a social world in which women are consistently devalued."

— Gaye Tuchman (1978), via Lind

"Groups that matter more in a culture have more power."

— Heldman & Bargiachi (2025)

The Extension

Framing & the Gaze

Lily's Post

Analyzed the slasher film's "punishing gaze" — how camera angles frame women's bodies as objects of threat rather than subjects of experience.

The Extension

Laura Mulvey's male gaze maps directly onto Iyengar's framing: the camera doesn't just record — it organizes meaning.

"Media does not directly tell us what to think, but instead tells us what to think about and how to think about it."

— Iyengar, via Heldman & Bargiachi

"Framing establishes the meaning of an event or activity."

— Erving Goffman (1974), via Lind

Two Ways to Study Media

Lind introduces a fundamental tension in the field — one your posts already lived out:

Social-Scientific
  • Experiments & surveys
  • Objective, value-neutral stance
  • Narrowly defined, measurable effects
  • E.g., does violent media increase aggression?
Cam's quantitative framing of tokenism draws on this tradition
Critical / Cultural Studies
  • Rhetorical & textual analysis
  • Explicitly political — seeks to end oppression
  • Cumulative, lifetime effects of representation
  • E.g., how does media perpetuate dominant ideology?
Lily's gaze analysis & Abbie's authorship question are rooted here
