Reading 1 · Blog Mickey, February 2025

What Disney Changed

In November 2024, Disney filed its annual SEC report — a legal document where every word is deliberate.

  • "Reimagine Tomorrow" removed from DEI section
  • "Heroes Work Here" — veteran hiring program — added in its place
  • Employee diversity fellowships scrapped
  • Inclusive appearance guidelines removed
  • DEI page rebranded as "Belong"
→ Visit Disney's "Belong" page
Why this is a legal story, not just a PR story

SEC filings carry regulatory and financial weight. Removing a program from an investor report is not a communications decision — it is a compliance and strategic one. Every word added or removed has legal consequences.

What does Disney gain by making its reporting legally defensible? What might it risk in the long term?

Reading 1 · The Documentary Record

History of Reimagine Tomorrow

2020
Disney announces the Reimagine Tomorrow initiative — a "large-scale platform for amplifying underrepresented voices."
2021
Program launches. The 2021 annual report describes it as building on Disney's "longstanding commitment to diversity, equity and inclusion." Revision of The Disney Look guidelines to embrace more inclusive appearance standards.
2022–23
Program continues to appear in annual SEC filings. Disney collaborates with advocacy groups to enhance LGBTQ+ representation in programming.
2024
November SEC filing: Reimagine Tomorrow is gone. The website is taken down. The program is replaced by Heroes Work Here.
Geena Davis Institute · On the original Reimagine Tomorrow film

"So how do we change what we see? At Disney, we start by acknowledging that the images we show and the stories we tell affect how people see themselves, how we see each other, and we haven't always gotten it right. But to Reimagine Tomorrow, we have to commit today to do all we can to use the power of storytelling, be honest about the world as it was, and reflect the world as it is — imagining the world as it can be to show the magic in every life. One complex, beautiful human story at a time."

Geena Davis Institute — Reimagine Tomorrow launch coverage

This film received over 3 million views. The website is now down. What does that tell us about the costs of erasure?

Reading 2 · Diversity.com · Competing Pressures

Who Pushed — and Why

🏛 Political Climate

Executive orders targeting DEI programs. Companies including Meta publicly ending their own initiatives. Disney reevaluating to align with "the evolving political and legal landscape."

📊 Activist Investors

Shareholders applauded the move as a return to "core business priorities." Investor pressure was a decisive factor in the rollback decision.

🤝 Advocates & Employees

Advocacy groups called the rollback a contradiction of Disney's brand. Employees expressed concerns about losing hard-won workplace inclusion progress.

If diverse content is profitable, how do you explain activist investor pressure winning? Whose interests are being prioritized?

Reading 3 · Variety · Industry-Wide Retreat

Disney Is Not Alone

Scaling Back

  • Disney — "Reimagine Tomorrow" removed
  • Amazon — walked back DEI hiring programs
  • Paramount — scaled back DEI embedding in hiring
  • Warner Bros. Discovery — "Diversity Digest" dropped
  • Google, Target, Accenture — reduced programs under pressure

Doubling Down

  • Apple — publicly reaffirmed DEI commitments
  • Costco — publicly doubled down on inclusion
  • Goldman Sachs — cited long-term benefits of inclusive workplace

What structural or financial differences explain why some companies can resist external pressure more successfully than others?
