The Basics

A legal instrument, not a press release.

Frances McDormand, 2018 Oscars — read the Hollywood Reporter explainer ↗
  • An inclusion rider is a clause above-the-line talent attach to their own contracts.
  • It requires productions to meet specific diversity benchmarks in casting and hiring.
  • Frances McDormand named it at the 2018 Oscars — the phrase went viral almost overnight.
  • A rider is binding. That's what separates it from a pledge or a PR statement.
Key Term 1 of 3

Hiring Pools

The pipeline you commit to before you hire.

The pool of candidates a production draws from when filling a role. A diverse hiring pool from the start makes a diverse final hire far more likely — it's a structural fix, not a hope.

Key Term 2 of 3

Benchmarks

The numbers that make it enforceable.

Numerical targets — e.g., a percentage of crew or background actors from underrepresented groups. Benchmarks turn aspiration into something measurable.

Caveat for Option B: a benchmark is only as good as what it measures. Hitting a number for background roles ≠ reshaping who leads a story.

Key Term 3 of 3

Accountability Measures

What happens if a production falls short.

The enforcement mechanism. Without it, a benchmark has no teeth.

Accountability measures are only as strong as the institution enforcing them. When that institution gets absorbed into a new parent company — who's still watching?

Discussion Option A

Accountability & Scale (The Business Lens)

Studio-Wide

  • Warner Bros.' 2018 company-wide policy
  • Broad reach across every production
  • Now absorbed into Paramount Skydance

Project-by-Project

  • Michael B. Jordan's Outlier Society
  • Smaller reach, but self-contained
  • Can't be erased by a corporate acquisition

Your task: does concentrating accountability in one big institution make it more powerful — or more fragile?

Discussion Option B

Intersectionality & Quality (The Creative/Data Lens)

  • Rider templates track gender, race, LGBTQIA+ identity, age, and disability — simultaneously.
  • Overlapping identities complicate data collection and benchmark-setting.
  • A benchmark per category can still miss someone at the intersection of several.

Your task: is hitting numerical benchmarks enough to dismantle decades of stereotyping — or is it a more formalized version of box-checking?
