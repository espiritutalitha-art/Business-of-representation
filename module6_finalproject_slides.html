Why Multimedia?

  • One of the core learning goals of this course is to communicate complex ideas about representation, diversity, and media business practices clearly and persuasively in both written and oral formats
  • Analysis is only as powerful as your ability to share it
  • A podcast or video asks you to distill your argument, speak to an audience, and make your ideas land — not just on paper, but out loud
  • This is a skill that matters well beyond this course

Choosing Your Path

  • Is there more you wanted to say in your Critical Analysis Paper that the format didn't allow? → Expanding it may be the natural move
  • Is there a media text or identity category from the second half of the course you're more excited about? → Starting fresh may serve you better
  • Either path is equally valid — choose the one that gives you the most to work with and the most to say
The best projects come from students who chose something they were genuinely invested in.

The Frameworks You'll Draw On

Quantitative

Population baselines, representation gaps, and screen time data from The Media Reader

Qualitative

Stereotype analysis, tropes, the male gaze, symbolic annihilation, the Ageless Test

Intersectional

How does your text handle the overlap of race, gender, age, disability, and sexuality?

Methodological

Close reading guided by the GDI creator questions, coding categories from Chonody et al., and other course frameworks
